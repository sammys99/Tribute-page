Tribute to Clair Cameron

Clair Cameron Patterson

A biochemist to talk about

Why is this biochemist important?

  • 2 of june 1922 -In Mitchellville, Iowa, born Clair
  • In 1953 he determined exactly the age of the Earth in 4,550 million years, with a margin of error of about 70 million years
  • In 1965 , Clair Patterson published the article "Polluted and natural environments of man"
  • Patterson died on December 5, 1995 due to an asthma attack

Patterson was one of the strongest opponents of the use of lead in the production of fuels, being persecuted for it by the multinationals that treated and commercialized it. Thanks to their efforts, the US Clean Air Act was passed in 1970.

In 1978 he was appointed to an NRC panel where he wrote a 78-page report stating that the application of control and measures against contamination by lead and other contaminants should begin immediately, including gasoline, gas containers food, paints, enamels and water distribution systems. Thirty years later, most are accepted in many parts of the world.
